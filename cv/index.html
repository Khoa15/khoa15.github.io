Image Retrieval

Khoa15/image-retrieval/blob/main/image-retrieval.ipynb

  • This project investigates the L1 distance method, which sums absolute pixel differences; its strength is simplicity and robustness to outliers, but its weakness is insensitivity to magnitude scale.
  • It explores the L2 distance method, computing Euclidean distance from squared differences; its strength lies in emphasizing larger deviations, though its weakness is higher sensitivity to outliers.
  • It examines cosine similarity, measuring vector angle for directional alignment; its strength is scale-invariance, but its weakness is ignoring magnitude entirely.
  • These methods differ fundamentally: L1 focuses on raw differences, L2 amplifies bigger gaps, and cosine prioritizes orientation over size, each with distinct trade-offs.
